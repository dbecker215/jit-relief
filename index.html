Just-In-Time Relief

In order to maximize the efficacy of disaster relief we need a “ground to crowd” (G2C) method of communication that allows people that are on the ground, with the victims of a disaster, to quickly identify and communicate what items are needed for those victims in specific locations. By setting up G2C communication channels our crowdsourced donations can be adapted to the actual needs on the ground instead of being based upon assumptions around what people should need in this type of situation. By successfully doing this we can then implement a “just-in-time” supply chain management philosophy that minimizes wasted time in warehouses, and reduces the workload of volunteers as they are only working on items that are immediately needed.

This website is a very rough minimum viable product (MVP) to begin executing. We will be looking for open-sourced help with building a refined product, but after we help the victims of Hurricane Harvey.
